Conchas

Description

A fabulous fabulous pandulce mexicano that everyone will love. Mmmm!

They're called "conchas" because they look like seashells. Or ... hmmm...

Ingredients

    Bread:

  • 1/2 cup warm water
  • 2 1/2 teaspoons yeast
  • 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup evaporated milk
  • 3/8 cup white sugar
  • 1/3 cup butter, melted
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon

    • Topping:

  • 2/3 cup white sugar
  • 1/2 cup butter, softened
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoons ground cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Steps

  1. Gather all of the ingredients.
  2. First we make the bread. Stir the warm water and yeast together, and let it stand until the yeast softens and begins to make a creamy foam, within 5 minutes or so.
  3. Mix in the 2 cups of flour, milk, sugar, melted butter, egg, and salt, till it's all combined.
  4. Gradually mix in the remaining 2 cups of flour and the cinnamon til the dough comes all together.
  5. Turn the dough out on a floured surface and knead it till it's smooth and elastic. Takes maybe 6 to 8 minutes. Then place it in a large greased bowl, turn the dough around to coat it.
  6. Cover this bowl with the dough and let it rise, in a warm place, for about 1 hour.
  7. Meanwhile, we gotta make the topping. Beat the sugar and butter in a medium bowl with an electric mixer until it's all light and fluffy.
  8. Stir in some of that flour until a thick paste forms.
  9. Transfer 1/2 of this paste to a separate bowl. Mix cinnamon in one of these, vanilla in the other one.
  10. Divide the dough into 12 equal pieces. Shape them into balls and place like 3 inches part on a greased cookie sheet.
  11. Then divide each bowl of topping into 6 balls, and pat them flat. Place the circles of toppings on each of the dough balls, pat them down lightly. Use a knife to cut grooves into the topping, making crazy seashell shapes o lo que sea.
  12. Cover these concoctions and let them rise until they're doubled in size, maybe 45 minutes or so.
  13. Preheat your oven to 375 degrees Fahrenheit.
  14. Bake these bad boys in the oven once it's ready to go for maybe 20 minutes, you're looking for them to hit a lightly golden brown color.
  15. BOOM. Bon appetit.
